3. Be Direct and Put Yourself Out There

Feeling imposter syndrome when joining a networking group? Don’t let it hold you back. Be direct: share who you are, where you’re at in your journey, and what you hope to gain.

People are willing to help if they understand your goals. Let them know what problem you’re solving in the marketplace so they remember your skills.

An easy way to stand out is by posting your progress. Share projects and work you’re proud of on LinkedIn and X as you learn. Hiring managers often find talent online, so regular posts about your projects, excitement, and skills boost your visibility.

Example: See this post by Deven McKay showcasing JavaScript skills.

Pro Tip: Surround yourself with people doing what you want to do. Accountants won’t help you become an actor—connect with tech professionals to grow.
